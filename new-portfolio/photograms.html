Emis Chen

Interactive Media Artist & Creative Coder

Black and White Photograms

Medium: Darkroom photogram on photographic paper

Dimensions: Each image approx. 8 x 10 in

Year: 2024

About this Work

These photograms were created in the darkroom by arranging objects directly on photographic paper and exposing them to light. The result is a unique composition of silhouettes and textures, exploring the interaction of light, transparency, and material. The layering of fabric and geometric motifs evokes both abstraction and personal narrative—inviting the viewer to interpret everyday forms in a new light. Each piece is original, not reproducible through digital means, and reflects the playful experimentation central to my practice.

This series represents a return to analog photographic processes, where the physical placement of objects creates direct impressions on light-sensitive paper. The work celebrates the unpredictable nature of traditional darkroom techniques while exploring themes of memory, presence, and absence.
